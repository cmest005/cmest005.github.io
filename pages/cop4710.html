COP4710: Databases

Description

Our Database class was taught using SQL and testing using MySQL through MySQL Workbench and phpMyAdmin. The intended outcome was to expose students to information systems and familiarize us with database systems and architectures. Some of the most interesting topics covered were: different joins, design schemas, create queries in SQL, relational algebra, and relational calculus.

Final Project

The goal of this project was to upload a file with 600k+ entries and write 4 queries to test for a correlation between 2 fields/attributes (one from the cars table, and one from any other source). Here is the creation of the cars table.

full page display

The direction was to come up with four queries which are interesting and can be connected in some way. Here are my 4 query topics:
  1. Query will give the average amount of savings on fuel costs (per mile) by consumers in FL who used the CARS program. The fuel PPG data will be from the prior 6 months to the program, and the following 6 months.
  2. Query will calculate the correlation coefficient between the amount of gas sold weekly during the 4 month CARS program, and the total cars sold weekly through the program.
  3. Query will display the correlation coefficient between the number of Toyotas bought during the CARS program and the number of Toyotas involved in fatal accidents per week in Florida.
  4. Query will calculate the ratio of Toyotas to Ford sold to the closing stock price of Toyota to Ford per week.
