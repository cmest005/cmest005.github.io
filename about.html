About me

My name is Carlos Andres Mestre. I am a 1st generation Cuban-American from Miami, Florida. I have a strong core of family and friends that are very important to me. Recently, in December 2016, I graduated from Florida International University with a B.A. in Computer Science. Working with computers has always been a strong passion of mine. Prior to earning my degree in Computer Science, I graduated from University of North Florida with a Bachelors in Business Administration. These two degrees give me a strong foundations for doing what I love, working as a programmer in a fast-paced environment that endorses teamwork.


I learned a lot while attending business school in North Florida. They had a couple of really interesting methods of structuring the classes. Every teacher I had was a current, or former business professional with lots of experience. Many of them were past CEOs, CFOs, etc. One thing that worried me for a little while, but I became a lot more comfortable with experience, was that every business class required team projects and presentation. It really showed how to work in a team effectively. Once I graduated, I did some temp jobs for a little while. I realized I just wasn't passionate enough for what I was doing. That's when I spoke to some of my friends and family and they really pushed me to go for a CS degree.


Admittedly, I started my CS degree with little to no experience with programming. I took a class in high school long before, and dabbled in some HTML, but what I was hit with was a shock. I truly struggled early on. I had to retake a few classes and contemplated maybe I had made a mistake. Again, my family and friends came with their support and I pushed on. It was a great decision. After a while it just start making sense. My grades starting going up a lot and eventually I felt very comfortable in my classes. A lot of credit also needs to go to the professors at FIU. They trully would go out of their way to help me outside of class. They just wanted to see the passion to work. So with these tools in hand, I feel I am ready for a successful career in something I enjoy doing.
